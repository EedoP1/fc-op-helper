## Root Cause Analysis

The optimizer's drop-and-backfill loop (line 76) fires when `len(selected) < 80`. With tiny test datasets (2-20 candidates), greedy fill always produces fewer than 80, so the loop:
1. Removes the most expensive selected player and BANS it permanently
2. Tries to backfill with cheaper players
3. With few candidates, this strips the portfolio

**6 Failing Tests and Why:**

1. `test_fills_budget` (20 players @ 10k, budget=50k): Greedy fills 5, drop-loop bans 1, result=4 not 5.
2. `test_no_duplicates` (1 unique player x5): Greedy fills 1, drop-loop bans it, result=0 not 1.
3. `test_prefers_higher_efficiency` (2 players, budget=15k): Greedy fills 1 (cheap), drop-loop bans it, result=0.
4. `test_swap_replaces_expensive_with_cheaper` (6 players): Drop-loop interferes, result=4 not 5.
5. `test_backfill_uses_remaining_budget` (2 players): Greedy fills big (90k), drop bans it, only small remains.
6. `test_ranks_by_expected_profit_per_hour` (2 players): Fills both (12k total), drop bans one.
